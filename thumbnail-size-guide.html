YouTube Thumbnail Size Guide 2026

Getting your thumbnail dimensions right is the first step to a professional-looking YouTube channel. Here's everything you need to know, straight from YouTube's own guidelines.

YouTube Thumbnail Specifications

Recommended size1280 × 720 pixels
Minimum width640 pixels
Aspect ratio16:9
Maximum file size2 MB
Accepted formatsJPG, PNG, GIF, BMP, WEBP

Why 1280×720?

YouTube displays thumbnails at many different sizes depending on the device and context — from small mobile grids (168×94px) to large desktop recommended feeds (640×360px). At 1280×720, your thumbnail has enough resolution to look sharp at any of these sizes.

Going larger doesn't help — YouTube compresses and resizes anyway. Going smaller risks blurry thumbnails on larger displays.

PNG vs JPG for thumbnails

Both work, but there are trade-offs:

  • PNG — lossless quality, slightly larger file size. Best for text-heavy thumbnails where sharpness matters.
  • JPG — smaller file size, some quality loss. Fine for photo-heavy thumbnails with soft gradients.

Thumbly exports PNG by default, which is the best choice for most thumbnails. Keep the file under 2MB or YouTube will reject it.

Font size for thumbnails

This is where most creators go wrong. Your thumbnail is often shown at 168×94px on mobile — that's tiny. Text needs to be enormous to be readable:

  • Main title: Use at least 100px font at 1280×720 (roughly 10% of canvas height)
  • Subtitle: Minimum 40px at 1280×720
  • Limit to 4–6 words: More text means smaller font means unreadable thumbnails

Thumbly auto-sizes Impact font to fill the canvas width — so your text is always as large as possible.

The "safe zone" rule

YouTube overlays play button icons, duration badges, and channel icons on your thumbnail. Keep important text and faces in the middle 80% of the canvas — avoid the bottom-left corner in particular (where the duration badge appears).
